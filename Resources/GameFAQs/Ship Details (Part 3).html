Czar


Strong against: Laser/Shuriken/Pellet, Lasers Only, Laser/Pellet, Laser/Shuriken
Weak against: Shuriken/MIRV

Ship Class: Double Defence
+

Main Weapon: Fission Slammer

  • Average DPS = 21.25
  • Target Tracking = Instant
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
    • (Invader Triggered - Synchronous)
  • Capacity = 2000ms
  • Reload Time = 1.8s (Slow)
  • Beam Width = 5
  • Instant tracking beam that locks on to a target point until the Invader is dead or after 2s of firing

Survivability (Daily Missions): B-
Survivability (Community Missions): B

  • (+) No need to care about aiming, so all you need is dodging.
  • (+) Stun EMP is used as per standard, either at the start of the wave (especially when there's lots of Sparrows !), or just before lasers and spinners are about to go off.
  • (+) Teleport helps you get in position to maximize a Stun EMP that is not full to hit as many turrets and Invaders as possible.
    • Teleport in the middle of the action and launch Stun EMP .
  • (+) While the Invaders are stunned, this allows you to move around with better freedom. Use this chance to try and maximize the Fission Slammer.
    • If the Fission Slammer is targets a bigger Invader such as a Heron or above, try to line it up with a Sparrow or Raven such that the smaller Invaders go down quickly.
    • Target switching for Czar takes time and you lose DPS.
  • The largest Invaders are always for last.
    • After Ravens , destroy Herons , then Eagles , and finally the Vulture .
  • Particle management is extremely important for Czar, as a bit lesser will result in lesser stun time which may mean you may run out of time to eliminate Invaders before they start firing again.
    • Don't allow Stun EMP Aura to overfill, fire it just before it can be full.
  • Circling around Vultures and Eagles is a very common technique for Czar so that you don't get trapped in one corner of the field.
  • You would want to start the next wave with at least more than half of a full size Stun EMP Aura , so that all Sparrows can be eliminated.
    • At the end wave, only large Invaders should remain.
    • Thus, try not to use Stun EMP to stun the large Invader unless it has very deadly turrets like MIRV Bloomers or Spreads.
  • (+) Teleport will then take care of the rest of your survival against larger Invaders.
  • (-) Unfortunately, Czar has a horrible targeting system which makes turret popping extremely unpredictable.
    • There is just absolutely no way you can direct the Fission Slammer to hit the turret you want.
  • (+) Use Stun EMP to reset spinners and MIRV Bloomers to cover for Czar's weakness of turret popping, even if the Stun EMP is pretty small.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): B-
Speedrunning (Community Missions): B

  • (-) The Fission Slammer's targeting is pretty much random.
    • However, there is a slight tendency to target whatever the Fission Slammer previously targeted before it stopped (either because the burst time has ended, or the Invader is destroyed).
    • Thus, it is a bit more likely to target something that is closer in terms of the angle of the last Fission Slammer.
  • (-) There's just no way you are speedrunning with the lowest DPS in the game, and Teleport doesn't do damage.
  • (+) Stun EMP does help to clear out Sparrows , but the Fission Slammer DPS is so low there's just no way this ship is going fast.

Fun Factor: B-

  • (+) Stun EMP wipes out a whole bunch of Sparrows .
  • (+) Easy ship core position.
  • (-) The Fission Slammer is easily the worst part of Czar since it's uncontrollable and trying to understand what is its next target is impossible.
  • (-) Czar is a ship that works, but is extremely boring to play.
  • (-) Boring Fission Slammer impact sounds.

Trivia

  • The base form of Czar was designed by Romano Molenaar, while the apexes were designed by Rogier van de Beek.
  • Czar used to have guns in the design of the pre-alpha version of Phoenix 2, as shown below:
  • Czar was named Skill UFO in a very early pre-alpha version of Phoenix 2 (that was never released to public).
  • At some point in time, Czar's Fission Slammer was two beams instead of one:
  • Czar used to be one of the most reliable ships to clear missions before the v3.5 update due to the powerful Stun EMP that lasted a whole 5 seconds. However, ever since the Stun EMP nerf, it has fallen out of meta.
  • Before the v4.0 update which introduced Apex forms, the Lambda Apex of Czar was teased on the Phoenix 2 Reddit:

Apexes

Lambda (Teleport Explosive Arrival) - 30.000


Value: S-
Cost vs Utility: S

Survivability (Daily Missions): B [+]
Survivability (Community Missions): B+ [+]

  • (+) The Stun EMP + Teleport Explosive Arrival combo immediately destroys Shielded Ravens . No more Ravens to deal with.
    • Any size of Stun EMP , even the smallest size works since Teleport Explosive Arrival deals 30 damage and Shielded Ravens have 31 health in total.
  • (+) Since Czar is quite free to go where it pleases thanks to Stun EMP , you dont really need Teleport when stun is in effect.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): A+ [+++]
Speedrunning (Community Missions): A+ [+++]

  • (+) In early Acts, use Teleport Explosive Arrival to get rid of Sparrows .
    • This prevents the Fission Slammer from keep on having to re-target something else, wasting precious time.
    • Use the time slowdown from the charged state of Teleport to wait for Sparrows to converge so that you can destroy 2 or 3 Sparrows in one Teleport.
  • (+) Using Stun EMP + Teleport Explosive Arrival on Ravens mean less re-targeting on the Fission Slammer, thus increasing effective DPS.
    • Similarly, wait for 2 or 3 Ravens to converge so that you can destroy 2 or 3 Ravens in one Teleport .
  • (+) Down the shields of Herons and Eagles first before using Teleport Explosive Arrival on them to maximize Fission Slammer Shield Breaker nature.
    • Get the Fission Slammer to target an Eagle , then swing around a Heron to have the Heron get some of the Fission Slammer damage.
    • Always prioritize Ravens and Sparrows first, don't Double Teleport if Teleport is taking more than one second to charge.
  • (+) Pop in a Teleport Explosive Arrival here and there on larger Invaders such as Vultures and above to deal some extra damage.
    • Be careful not to overwork Teleport to the point where it is difficult to use Teleport Explosive Arrival on the next wave.
    • Allow Teleport to recover when left with one huge Invader.

Fun Factor: A+ [+++]

  • (+) Using Stun EMP + Teleport Explosive Arrival on Ravens mean less frustration from the unpredictable Fission Slammer targeting.
  • (+) The Stun EMP + Teleport Explosive Arrival to wipe out Ravens is very fun.

Delta (Double Teleport ) - 20.000


Value: C+
Cost vs Utility: B

Survivability (Daily Missions): B- [No significant change]
Survivability (Community Missions): B- [No significant change]

  • (+) Increases defence against MIRVs .
  • (+) Using the smallest size of Stun EMPs , you can Teleport near a deadly spinner before it starts to fire, fire Stun EMP, then quickly Teleport back to safety.
    • With a small bit of grazing, it's easy to always fire the smallest Stun EMP .
  • (+) Allows you to confuse dart spreads.
    • Use the first Teleport to an upper corner of the field.
    • Wait for the dart spreads to fire.
    • Then Teleport back down and the downfield will be a bit more manageable.
  • (-) Using an Stun EMP after the first Teleport will cancel the time slowdown from Teleport.
    • This means that after allowing the second Teleport to charge, you'll need to Teleport back down very quickly.
    • Otherwise the second Teleport will cancel very quickly due to the canceled time slowdown.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): B- [No change]
Speedrunning (Community Missions): B [No change]

Fun Factor: B- [No significant change]

  • (+) Double Teleport on Czar has the interesting application of stun resetting any desired turret(s).
  • (-) However, due to the conflict between a second Teleport slowdown and Stun EMP , this can be difficult to pull off.

Havoc


Strong against: None in particular
Weak against: Shuriken/MIRV, Dart/MIRV

Ship Class: Full Offence (FO)
+

Main Weapon: Mass Driver

  • Average DPS = 31.25
  • Blast Damage = 2 (Low)
  • Blast Radius = 80
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
  • Rate of Fire = 0.1s (Fast)
  • Projectile Speed = 1100 (Very Fast)
  • Reload Time = 0.5s (Medium)
  • Projectile Hit Circle Radius = 5
  • 4 successive forward firing projectiles in a slight alternating left-right

Survivability (Daily Missions): D
Survivability (Community Missions): D

  • (-) No defense other than Mega Laser .
  • (+) Decent turret popping capability.
  • (-) However, it is difficult to get into position to pop turrets, given very little cues on when the next burst of Mass Driver is going to fire.
    • It takes some experience with Havoc to catch its rhythm which is not easy.
  • Use an Laser Storm at the end of a wave so that you can begin charging Mega Laser while Laser Storm does its job.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): A
Speedrunning (Community Missions): A-

  • (+) Chaining Laser Storm after Mega Laser at the start of a wave works to take out either Vultures or one side of the Invaders rather quickly.
  • (-) Havoc doesn't take out Invaders that quickly.
    • Since it's also difficult to position yourself to fire Mega Laser to hit the Invader cockpits, Mega Laser often doesn't deal the full damage to Armored Invaders.
  • (-) Havoc's slightly unpredictable cooldown between Mass Driver bursts makes it difficult to estimate the best time to start firing Laser Storm for the next wave.
    • This is because it's difficult to use one last burst of Mass Driver before letting Laser Storm finish off the wave.
    • In particular, the somewhat unpredictable timing the Mass Driver means it's difficult to hit the remaining Ravens in the wave.
  • (-) Since the blast damage of the Mass Driver is quite insignificant, you would often need to either take out the remaining Sparrows with the Mass Driver or use Laser Storm to do so.
    • As mentioned, taking out Sparrows with the Mass Driver is difficult, and even more so with smaller Sparrows.
  • (+) 2 Mega Lasers in a heavy wave may be possible, so always look out for such opportunities.
    • However, the awkwardness of Havoc's Mass Driver may make transiting to the other side difficult.
  • (+) In lighter waves, use Laser Storm to finish off the rest of the wave and charge Mega Laser early.
    • Just one full burst of the Mass Driver would do, then deploy Laser Storm and start charging Mega Laser .
  • Mega Laser becomes fully cooled down after 4 cycles of the Mass Driver.

Fun Factor: A-

  • (+) Going fast can indeed be quite fun, although Havoc isn't per say very fast.
  • (-) The Mass Driver is pretty annoying to use, being bursty and thin.
  • (-) Difficult to determine the cooldown interval of Havoc, unless you play with Havoc a lot. Havoc is a difficult ship to get used to.
  • (-) Havoc's ship core position is very awkward being towards the back of the ship.
    • The shape of Havoc is another massive hindering factor to piloting it.
  • (=) Average Mass Driver impact sounds. It's kind of in-between sounding powerful and sounding average.

Trivia

  • The base form of Havoc was designed by Romano Molenaar, while the apexes were designed by Rogier van de Beek.
  • Here's an early sketch version of Havoc:
  • From the initial drawing of Havoc above, the dev immediately fell in love with it.
    • However, when it was sent for coloring, it lost a lot of the power of the original design.
    • The dev then tried to give it a cool strong weapon, but it never really worked out.
  • Here's how an early pre-alpha version of Havoc looked like (that was never released to public):
  • At some point in time, Havoc's Mass Driver was of an Interruptible Burst class. It was then changed to the current Uninterruptible Burst weapon class.
  • At the v5.5 update, the Blast damage for Havoc's Mass Driver is no longer scaled according to main weapon level. This means the blast damage no longer reduces at higher main weapon levels.

Geist


Strong against: Dart/Pellet, Dart/Boomerang/Laser, Dart/Pellet/Laser, Laser/Shuriken/Pellet, Lasers Only, Laser/Pellet, Laser/Shuriken
Weak against: Shuriken/MIRV, Dart/MIRV, Shuriken/Boomerang/MIRV

Ship Class: Offensive
+

Main Weapon: Quantum Blast

  • Average DPS = 37.5
  • Spread = -15 to 15 (Wide)
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
  • Rate of Fire = 0.8s (Fast)
  • Projectile Speed = 850 (Medium)
  • Reload Time = 1.6s (Slow)
  • Projectile Hit Circle Radius = 3
  • 3 successive sets of 8 diverging projectiles fired in a general forward direction

Survivability (Daily Missions): B-
Survivability (Community Missions): B-

  • (-) Very little turret popping capability. Kill Invaders in their entirety.
  • (+) Easy to understand Quantum Blast.
    • It even has visual cues to tell you when it is going to fire so you can position yourself properly.
  • (-) Personal Shield is its only defence.
    • Use Personal Shield early in the wave against boomerangs where necessary, usually in the first burst.
    • All boomerang turrets to go off at the same time.
    • Then, after the boomerangs are blocked off, use Personal Shield Fade Out to push forward so you can avoid subsequent bursts of boomerangs.
  • (+) However, Personal Shield deals very well against lasers .
    • It's a different story for lasers compared to boomerangs .
    • You would want to go in close to deal damage first, then retreat downfield and let all the lasers come and find you.
  • (-) Difficult to deal against MIRVs .
    • Personal Shield is the only defence against them, and rapidly firing MIRV Spreads will cause a huge problem.
    • Unfortunately, you just have to kill that offending Invader quickly.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): S-
Speedrunning (Community Missions): A+

  • (+) Geist absolutely wrecks Rocs due to its high Quantum Blast DPS combined with damage from Missile Swarm .
    • Obviously, in the presence of heavy laser fire, Geist does even more damage.
    • Destroy the initial wave of Invaders before the Roc arrives with an Missile Swarm .
      • Grab their particles for a full Missile Swarm Aura for the Roc and leave particles floating around from the previous wave.
      • When the Roc arrives, launch the Missile Swarm , and rush forward to grab the particles you left behind and immediately fire a second Missile Swarm.
      • The Roc will go down in no time, particularly if the Roc has lasers to use Personal Shield for additional laser reflection damage.
  • In early Acts, especially on waves with a lot of Sparrows , stand back so that the Quantum Blast can catch all the Sparrows.
    • There's a fair bit of RNG involved so it'll not be always perfect.
  • (+) A small size Missile Swarm (up to about half size) will take care of lighter waves.
    • The Quantum Blast doesn't always need to hit something as it can be difficult to do so on lighter waves.
  • (+) Since Geist's Quantum Blast is an uninterruptible burst, a good combo is to let the Quantum Blast be primed (when the Quantum Blast is about to fire, you can see a pinkish glow in front of the ship), and then deploy Personal Shield .
    • The Quantum Blast continues to fire while the Personal Shield reflects lasers .
    • Just be careful not to deploy Personal Shield too late, otherwise you will die from either bullets or lasers .
  • Remember that Geist rewards aggression.
    • Getting up close to Invaders as far as possible to minimize DPS leakage.
    • Use Personal Shield if you need to push upwards to get up close to Invaders.
  • In heavier and/or dense waves, it is generally not too much of an issue to stand back.
  • Try to finish off waves with an Missile Swarm .
    • This is because if the waves has only a few Ravens and/or Sparrows , it is difficult to use the Quantum Blast to get close to them.
    • Getting close to them also means going upfield and running the huge risk of overfilling your Missile Swarm Aura .
    • If this is not avoidable, it's better to simply use the Quantum Blast to finish them off instead of Missile Swarm as there will be too much overkill damage with Missile Swarm.

Fun Factor: S

  • (+) Geist has the highest potential damage per main weapon burst, coming in at 70-75 damage, not accounting for critical hits.
  • (+) Personal Shield is very fun to use against laser heavy missions, and to watch Invaders destroy themselves really just puts a smile on your face.
  • (+) The highly bursty Quantum Blast is really fun to use, due to the comfortable spread , which means that you are almost guaranteed to hit some Invader no matter what.
  • (+) Very satisfying Quantum Blast impact sounds.
  • (-) Geist ship core position can be a little awkward, partially because of its dark green design.

Trivia

  • The base form of Geist was designed by Romano Molenaar, while the apexes were designed by Rogier van de Beek.
  • In a pre-alpha version of Phoenix 2 showcased in GDC, Geist was showcased as a ship with a main weapon that was the generic weapon for all ships.
    • The main weapon seemed to become stronger the longer you used Geist without using the Aura .
    • Using the Aura would reset Geist's main weapon back to its initial state.
    • Click to watch the gameplay of pre-alpha Geist at GDC (Fast forward the video to the 6:50 mark).
  • Geist's Quantum Blast used to be Shield Breaker before it was changed to High Impact in the v3.0 update.
  • As a teaser to the apex update (v4.0), the devs released a teaser screenshot on Discord.
  • Geist was supposed to get an apex that was chrome in color. Unfortunately, the devs felt it did not fit Geist since the apex lost the feel of Geist altogether, although they feel it was "very slick". Only a small part of this scrapped chrome apex was shown to the Discord.
  • At v6.0, the screenshake brought upon by the impact of Quantum Blast was reduced. In fact, the screenshake of the Quantum Blast had been reduced in a previous update as well.

Juggernaut


Strong against: Shuriken/Pellet, Shuriken/MIRV, Dart/MIRV
Weak against: Dart/Boomerang/Laser, General - No Pellets, Dart/Pellet/Laser, Lasers Only, Laser/Pellet, Laser/Shuriken

Ship Class: Attack & Retreat
+

Main Weapon: Devastator Cannon

  • Average DPS = 31.25
  • Blast Damage = 2.1 (Low)
  • Blast Radius = 140
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
  • Projectile Speed = 1100 (Very Fast)
  • Reload Time = 1.15s (Slow)
  • Projectile Hit Circle Radius = 5
  • 2 tightly packed side-by-side forward firing projectiles

Survivability (Daily Missions): S-
Survivability (Community Missions): S-

  • (+) Bullet EMP gives it a lot of survivability in Dart/MIRV and Shuriken/MIRV missions, where bullets get extremely dense.
  • (+) You can launch multiple Bullet EMPs to keep on clearing the field.
    • Particularly, make sure you launch a Bullet EMP just before or when the current wave ends, so that you can reliably set up Mega Bomb for the next wave.
  • (+) After Mega Bomb goes off, look for larger Invaders to land the Devastator Cannon on, as the heavily damaged Ravens from the Mega Bomb will get destroyed by the blast damage of the Devastator Cannon.
  • If there are no large Invaders, try to aim slightly offset the mid-line of the field so that the blast damage will eliminate as many Ravens on that side of the field.
  • (+) The powerful Devastator Cannon can wipe out Ravens when both projectiles land on it, as well as Sparrows nearby.
    • A Sparrow needs 2 blast damage from the Devastator Cannon projectiles.
    • Thankfully, the blast radius of the Devastator Cannon is quite big.
  • (+) A single well landed Devastator Cannon burst can destroy a Vulture's turret, meaning both projectiles hit the turret.
    • This is especially useful to take out MIRV Bloomers or Spreads.

    • Prioritize the elimination of MIRV Bloomers if they exist, otherwise, kill the Ravens with rapid firing MIRV launchers.
    • If there is not enough Aura to eliminate MIRVs after the shuriken cloud has dispersed, simply stay on the side with lesser bullets and activate Kappa Drive to generate Aura for you.
      • This is faster than diving right in and exploding MIRVs since you don't need to dodge the pellets released.
  • Use Kappa Drive between waves, or when you need more Aura to eliminate bullets to escape a tight spot.
  • Because you simply camp there, particles come in very slowly.
    • You can sometimes spam Point Defence and move upfield a bit to collect the particles if the shuriken cloud isn't terribly thick, which eliminates the shuriken cloud, MIRVs and other stray bullets within it.
  • (-) There's just no way to deal with lasers at all.
    • A wave with plenty of lasers is just Vuxine's doom.
    • You can only hope to use Point Defence strategically to cut a hole in the bullet lanes and then Kappa Dodge.
  • (-) The lackluster DPS of Vortex Launcher makes it hard to clear waves so you have to survive wave after wave of bullets and lasers.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): D
Speedrunning (Community Missions): D

Photurius


Strong against: Dart/Pellet, Shuriken/Pellet, Dart/Boomerang/Pellet, Shuriken/Boomerang/MIRV, Dart/Boomerang/Laser, General - No Pellets, General - No Rangs, Laser/Shuriken/Pellet
Weak against: Shuriken/MIRV, Dart/MIRV

Ship Class: Lightfury/Close Combat
+

Main Weapon: Photon Charge

  • Average DPS = 34.375
  • Spread = -12.5 to 12.5 (Wide)
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
  • Projectile Speed = 1500 (Very Fast)
  • Reload Time = 1s (Slow)
  • Projectile Hit Circle Radius = 8
  • 6 very fast projectiles fired from the front with a slow divergence rate

Survivability (Daily Missions): A-
Survivability (Community Missions): A-

  • Always attempt to pull off an Mega Laser at the start of a medium (or above) wave.
  • Otherwise, Stun EMP should always be fired immediately no matter what, even though there may be Vultures with spinners at the back.
    • By killing fast, you refill your Aura quickly enough (from downfield Invaders) to fire off the next Stun EMP just as the spinners at the back are about to fire.
  • Stun EMP should be quickly followed by an Mega Laser if you are unable to pull off an Mega Laser at the start of the wave.
  • (-) Due to its wide spread Photon Charge, Photurius is unable to pop turrets off.
    • Good thing is, you usually don't even need to do that, since Photurius kills so fast.
  • (+) Consistent Photon Charge which is quite easy to handle.
  • (-) However, the wide spread of the Photon Charge is quite difficult to land properly, especially on Ravens which require the full burst of Photon Charge to eliminate them after being stunned.
  • Photurius can be quite effective to pop off difficult-to-reach turrets on Rocs .
    • For a Roc , you want to fire Stun EMP as you dash forward and make sure that all the turrets are stunned.
    • Then, collect particles on the way up.
    • You would want to position yourself on one side, between 2 of the Roc's difficult-to-reach turrets.
    • It takes 2 bursts of the Photon Charge to eliminate them both. After which, you want to launch a 2nd Stun EMP .
    • Now head over to the other side of difficult-to-reach turrets to pop them off.
    • At the end of the process, all the back turrets of the Roc should be gone.
      • Watch out for the front row turrets if they can turn behind fast enough to aim at you.
  • This process won't be necessary on Condors , as mentioned above.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): S
Speedrunning (Community Missions): S

  • (+) The highly damaging Photon Charge, coupled with Mega Laser means Photurius is really fast.
  • (+) A full burst of Photon Charge eliminates an Armored Raven .
    • After stunning them all, go right to the Ravens' cockpits to land all projectiles of a burst.
  • Use the time slowdown after firing off an Stun EMP to reposition yourself, as this allows one burst of Photon Charge to go off before charging Mega Laser .
  • In early waves, stand back to reach all the Ravens and Sparrows .
    • After all the Sparrows are dead, use Stun EMP (if necessary) and move forward to eliminate the Ravens .
  • In heavier and/or dense waves, you should fire a 2nd Mega Laser .
    • Start charging the 2nd Mega Laser after you launch the 1st Stun EMP .
    • A general guide is this is necessary for 4 Eagle waves, or anything with an equivalent total health or higher.
    • 3 Eagles is too few and would transit to the next wave too quickly.
    • 2 Eagles and 2 Herons are also not enough since one Mega Laser is just able to take out 1 Eagle and 1 Heron (if the Mega Laser lands on the Eagle's cockpit, which is quite difficult).
  • (+) The freedom to move around after firing Stun EMP allows you to reposition yourself.
    • You can reposition yourself in front of an Eagle's or Vulture's cockpit to deal maximum damage such that no Photon Charge projectiles are wasted.
  • Because Photurius' Photon Charge is pretty infrequent, fake charge Mega Laser in the transition to a light wave so that you don't waste the powerful bursts of Photon Charge.
  • (+) A third Mega Laser in the same wave is not needed due to the high DPS of Photon Charge. Even for waves with a Roc .
  • If you have used 2 Mega Lasers in the previous wave, the next wave should have at most only one Mega Laser.
    • Otherwise, the Charge Times will get too long and you waste too much time.
  • (+) One Mega Laser , one Stun EMP , 2 bursts of Photon Charge, and finally one more Mega Laser is enough to obliterate a Condor , provided both Mega Lasers are aimed at the Condor's cockpit.
    • You can start charging the second Mega Laser the moment the second burst of Photon Charge is primed.
      • This gives the Condor no chance to even pour out bullets into the field.
    • Be careful of the other Invaders around though.
    • This case is generally ideal for a Condor , backed up by 4 Ravens behind it since the Ravens are too far away to do anything to you once stunned.
    • If there are Eagles in front of the Condor , if they can't aim backwards, you can go straight for the Condor first.
      • However, remember that you will likely exhaust your Stun EMP after destroying the Condor .
      • Since the particles provided by a Condor are very heavy, it'll be difficult to stun the Eagles and go back downfield if the Eagles in front have deadly middle turrets.
  • Depending on how deadly the Roc is, you may have to employ the technique above to get rid of the difficult-to-reach turret on a Roc.
    • However, if the difficult-to-reach turrets are not deadly enough, it is better to stay downfield and charge a second Mega Laser somewhere mid into the Roc battle so that Mega Laser has time to recover properly.
    • You will also save particles for the next wave if you do not go upfield to pop off the difficult-to-reach turrets.

Fun Factor: X

  • (+) Photurius has a very satisfying Photon Charge that goes POOF.
  • (+) Easy to handle Photon Charge. It's easy to know when it'll go off.
  • (+) Instant elimination of Armored Ravens if all of the Photon Charge hits them.
  • (+) Extremely high adrenaline gameplay, requiring you to go right up to the Invaders' face to deal damage.
    • Very satisfying gameplay loop: Fire Mega Laser , ride up Mega Laser, fire Stun EMP , blast Invaders in their face, fire Stun EMP again before stun expires, blast Invaders in their face.
    • Repeat until all Invaders are dead, then go back down to charge Mega Laser for the next wave.
  • (+) The highly spammable nature of Stun EMP and the highly damaging Photon Charge makes gameplay very fast paced and very fun.
  • (+) Continuous stun with Stun EMP due to the high damage output from Mega Laser and Photon Charge. This means Invaders are disabled most of the time, which is honestly laughable.
  • (+) Continuous firing of Stun EMP also means a lot of AoE damage .

Xaniea


Strong against: Dart/Pellet, Shuriken/Pellet, Dart/Boomerang/Pellet
Weak against: Dart/Boomerang/Laser, Dart/Pellet/Laser, Lasers Only, Laser/Pellet, Laser/Shuriken

Ship Class: Offensive
+

Main Weapon: Photon Pods

  • Average DPS = 34.375
  • Spread = -20 to 20 (Wide)
  • Weapon Class = Uninterruptible Burst
  • Rate of Fire = 0.15s (Fast)
  • Projectile Speed = 800 to 200 with a deceleration of 400 (Medium)
  • Reload Time = 0.6s (Medium)
  • Projectile Hit Circle Radius = 4
  • 5 successive sets of 7 projectiles fired in a general forward direction with a moderate divergence rate

Survivability (Daily Missions): B-
Survivability (Community Missions): B-

  • (+) While Xaniea does rely on killing fast, it has Teleport , it's saving grace in times of being threatened.
  • (+) Easy to manage Photon Pods with a consistent cooldown time between bursts.
  • (+) Teleport helps allow Laser Storm to reach a target if you require to kill something urgently but don't have a big size Laser Storm Aura .
  • (-) Unfortunately, the Photon Pods wide spread means that it is unable to focus fire, thus turret popping is very difficult, so taking out boomerangs is difficult.
    • Laser Storm does cover this flaw slightly, but due to the nature of Laser Storm to switch targets rather quickly, it's not entirely reliable.
  • What you can do to partially overcome this flaw of turret popping is to deploy Laser Storm first, and then go point blank to the turret you want to pop off.
    • When the dangerous turret fires, retreat.
    • However, it is difficult to eliminate turrets quickly enough before spread turrets force you to retreat.
  • Because lasers in General - No Pellets missions can track rather fast, Teleport is also a means to escape these lasers by Teleport-ing to the opposite side.
    • This is natural as you would start off a wave a bit more upfield and then retreat towards the bottom of the screen.
    • Offset slightly to one side so that not too much DPS is being lost.

Speedrunning (Daily Missions): A-
Speedrunning (Community Missions): A-

  • (+) The rather high damaging Photon Pods, coupled with Laser Storm , means Xaniea goes reasonably fast.
  • (+) Use Teleport to get up close to Invaders after being pushed back from bullets.
    • Think of it as leaping past the wave of bullets.
    • This works really well for any kind of bullet spreads.
  • (+) Before you charge Teleport , let the burst of Photon Pods go off so you minimize downtime.
  • Use Laser Storm whenever possible.
  • (-) A bit difficult to deal against Sparrows since the Photon Pods aren't exactly very powerful.
    • The assistance of Laser Storm is ideal to get rid of them.
  • Start each wave by being as upfield as possible.
    • Leave a bit of gap between you and the Invaders so you can respond to threats.
    • Retreat downfield as spread turrets fire. This allows you not to only maximize the Photon Pods, but also potentially pop off turrets.
    • If the turrets are boomerangs , that's even better because they will curve around you.

Fun Factor: A+

  • (+) Laser Storm is rather aesthetically pleasing and has pretty satisfying sounds to couple with it.
  • (+) Together with the rather aesthetically pleasing Photon Pods, it is a visually appealing ship to play.
  • (-) Unfortunately, this also means that the visuals of the Xaniea's offensive means might make it hard to see and survive.
  • (=) Average Photon Pod impact sounds. It kind of sounds like a washing machine.
  • (+) Easy to handle Photon Pods.
  • (+) Very kinetic ship, meaning quite a lot of movement. Particularly, pushing forward and then retreating is a nice momentum.
